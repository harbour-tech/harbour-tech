Fees and Conversion Rates

You can display live pricing and conversion rates to customers for converting supported fiat currencies to supported crypto assets with The EstimateOnrampFee and EstimateOfframpFee APIs.

The rates and fees shown include:

Conversion Rates​

The rate Harbour will convert a supported fiat currency for a supported crypto asset (and vice versa).

The conversion rates displayed by Harbour are always accurate at the time of quoting. The precise rate a Customer receives is determined at the exact time a payment is received from the Customer (by either sending a Supported Fiat Currency to their Magic IBAN or a supported crypto asset to their Magic Wallet).

Harbour directly passes on to Customers the conversion rates it receives from institutional liquidity and exchange partners. Unlike many other web 3 payments and ramp services, we do not add any additional ‘spread’ on the rates we receive for buying / selling supported crypto assets from institutional partners.

Network Fees​

Only applies to fiat to crypto conversions, as network fees are paid by users in their own wallet when converting crypto to fiat.

Network fees (aka gas fees) are costs of processing the transfer of supported crypto assets on a supported blockchain. Such fees vary between blockchains and can also fluctuate based on network congestion.

Unlike many other web 3 payments and ramp services, we do not add any margin onto the network fees we pay to process transactions, although the exact amount is still estimated to the best of our ability.

Processing Fees​

Processing fees are Harbours fees for processing transactions.

Referral Fees​

Referral Fees (aka Partner Fees) are fees that can be set by you, to be charged to your customers. Should you wish to charge a referral fee, it will be included in your contract with Harbour during onboarding.
