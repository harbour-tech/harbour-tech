Additional considerations​

Ramp Status​

The GetAccountInfo API returns a ramp status for both on- and off-ramping (buying and selling crypto). The status can have a zero value (RAMP_STATUS_UNSPECIFIED), which means that the crypto asset is tradable and well functioning on our platform.

In most cases, we'll be able to serve customers within seconds. In rare occasions our system might be experiencing delays, and in exceptional cases, we might even halt trading a specific asset temporarily. In those scenarios, the ramp status will either be RAMP_STATUS_DEGRADED or RAMP_STATUS_HALTED.

It is important to implement detection of these statuses and rendering of the appropriate UI elements to inform the end user.

Account Limits​

It is equally important for the client app to be aware of the account limits, of which there's two kinds:

  • An absolute limit, meaning that no single transaction can exceed it.
  • A daily limit, taking into consideration the cumulative 24h volume for that account (user).

Both are expressed in the user's base currency (e.g. EUR in Europe, GBP in the UK, etc.). When asking the user for the amount to buy or sell, the client app should display and enforce those limits, based on the information provided by the GetAccountInfo API, within the CryptoAsset message.
